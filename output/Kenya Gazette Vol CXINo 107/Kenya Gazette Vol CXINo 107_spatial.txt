Terms of Reference for the Working Group

To guide, facilitate and oversee the development of an effective working system on BPO/ITES related activities within the various government ministries that will result in an organized and cohesive co-ordination of efforts towards meeting 'the objectives of Vision 2030 for the BPO/ITES sector.

To Facilitate and oversee the development and achievement of high level interaction and negotiations with domestic, regional and international partners for facilitation of industry growth.

To monitor and advice provision of infrastructure for the BPO/ITES sector through the Office of the Prime Minister.

To oversee the process to actively generate new business and investment in the sector through facilitating "tent pitching" of foreign investors by direct one to one meetings with senior level outsourcing decision makers in Global 1000 companies.

To facilitate, co-ordinate and oversee the development of a strategy for Rural BPO/ITES efforts between various ministries towards effecting adequate sector growth in rural/suburban areas so as to

Published by Authority of the Republic of Kenya

stem rural-urban migration as well as encourage development in smaller towns and rural areas.

To guide, facilitate and oversee the development of a monitoring and evaluation framework that will assess the general status on the development of the sector.

To monitor and evaluate the impact of BPO/ITES related activities based on key milestones and objectives.

Powers of the Working Group

The Working Group may establish committees to address technical or other specific issues. The Working C)Irctip will prepare the Terms of Reference of its Committees. The membership of the committees will draw from relevant institutions, particularly: Ministry of Information and Communication, Ministry of Trade, Ministry of Science and Technology and Ministry of Education, Vision 2030 Secretariat, Brand Kenya Board, E-Govemment, ICT Board, Kenya Investment Authority, Export Processing Zones Authority and KEPSA among others.

The , Working Group and its committees may invite external experts to participate on an ad hoc basis in one or more of their meetings.
